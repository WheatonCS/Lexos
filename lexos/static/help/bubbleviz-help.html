The BubbleViz Tool

BubbleViz offers an alternative to word clouds as a method of visualizing the Document-Term Matrix. They present terms arranged inside circles ("bubbles") sized according to the terms' frequency within the text. BubbleViz graphs enable you to get a sense of the content in your corpus, and they are very good for presentations.

Usage

The BubbleViz tool will automatically create a graph with all the active documents. To use less or more of the documents in your corpus, visit the Manage tool to activate and deactivate documents.

  • Word Count

    • Run your mouse cursor over a term to open a tooltip showing the number of times the term occurs in your selected document(s).

Options

Remember to click "Generate" again to see your changes reflected in the BubbleViz graph.

  • Stop Words

    • By default, Lexos does not exclude any terms from the BubbleViz graph. But you can scrub stop words like "the", "and", "a" under the Scrub tool to remove these words from your visualization.

  • Font

    • You can change the appearance of your word cloud by setting the font here. This feature should work with any font installed on your system.

  • Term Count

    • By default, Lexos includes the top 100 terms in your documents. Use this setting to modify the number. Limiting the number of terms may help you to include high frequency terms which are dropped by the layout algorithm.

  • Color

    • The default color scheme is Blue and Grey, but you can click here to select different colors for your visualization.

  • Download

    • Word clouds are downloadable in either .svg or .png format. SVG images are very useful because they scale well in web browsers.
